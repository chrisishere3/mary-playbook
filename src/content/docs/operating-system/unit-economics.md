How money actually flows from a partner. Three drivers, one cost structure, two margin numbers, and the math you'll use constantly.

## The three drivers of revenue

1. **Purchase rate.** ~25% of addresses generate an internet purchase. Healthy and scales.
2. **Revenue per purchase.** ~$200. Sustainable. Varies by month based on which telecom got selected and what speed tier.
3. **Revenue share with PM.** Lower than originally planned. PMs are happy with the split today. Competition could pressure this.

## Cost structure

- Team in Mexico handles mapping (making sure every address has the right utilities), placing orders, and support.
- These costs scale down as a % of revenue over time.

## Margins

- **Current:** ~60%
- **Steady state:** 75–85%

## Doors

"Doors" = homes under management. PMs bill landlords based on doors. This is the unit you'll use for sizing partners, channel ROI, and forecasting.

## Estimating PM size without asking

Active listings × 14 ≈ total doors.

Not perfect (absorption rates vary by market), but directionally right and lets you size a prospect from public listing data alone.

## Leases per month math

- Average lease length: **30 months**. About half of tenants renew.
- A home comes back on the market roughly **once every 30 months**.
- 400-door PM = 400 / 30 = **~13 leases/month**.

## Revenue per partner

```
Monthly revenue = (doors / 30) × $200 × 0.25
```

Where 0.25 is the purchase rate.

Rule of thumb (Zach's): **at current conversion and turnover rates, we earn ~$1 in recurring monthly revenue per door activated.** Activate 20K doors in a month → expect ~$20K revenue in following months.

## Where there's more room

- **Higher gross profit margin.** Operational efficiency on the mapping/support side compounds. Margin lift is a direct LTV lift.
- **Higher revenue per partner.** Add new monetization layers (other utilities, residual renter products, the vacancy/move-out workflow if we crack it).
- **Better purchase rate.** Today ~25%. Anything we do to make the renter experience smoother or default-on lifts this directly.
