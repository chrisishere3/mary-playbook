The marketing function carries several real, unresolved strategic disagreements. This page surfaces them rather than burying them, because resolving them is part of the VP role.

## The vacancy product thesis

The argument from the marketing team:

- SFR and garden-style PMs care about utilities at **move-out**, not just move-in
- The current product is move-in focused; the next logical product is vacancy / move-out
- No competitor is focused on vacancy
- Existing channels are saturated; new positioning, audiences, or channels will not unlock incremental growth at the current product
- **Therefore:** product differentiation through vacancy features is required for second-half growth

The case has been made to Ben and Darius:

- **Darius** is more open to it
- **Ben** believes the focus should be D2C, where the market is 50x bigger

Product capacity is currently zero-sum. The product team will not work on vacancy because OnSwitch (auto-enrollment) and other higher-priority work consume capacity. **OnSwitch is correctly identified as the most important long-term product bet** — it unlocks downstream functionality across all segments.

**The unresolved tension:** OnSwitch is the right long-term bet, but it does not serve SFR/GS acquisition specifically. Vacancy would. Capacity to build both does not exist.

The VP role has more credibility and latitude to advocate for vacancy than the senior ICs have.

## D2C vs. SFR/GS resource allocation

Ben prioritizes D2C because it is a 50x larger market. The marketing team agrees with the prioritization in principle but believes it costs SFR/GS market capture.

The reconciled position: both matter; the answer is more capacity in product and sales, not picking one over the other. The hiring of a VP of Marketing is part of that capacity expansion.

**The implicit risk:** if fundraising does not produce additional product and sales capacity, the company will functionally prioritize D2C and SFR/GS will slow. The 7.8% → 25% market share goal becomes less realistic. Nobody has said this out loud.

## The market share denominator question

UP only actively pursues PMs with **100 or more doors**. The long tail (PMs managing 50 doors or fewer) is the majority of the SFR/GS PM market by count, but has no acquisition motion.

The real addressable market within SFR/GS is roughly 25% of the total segment.

**The open question: what is the denominator of the 7.8% → 25% market share goal?**

- If 25% is "of total SFR + GS PM market" — the long tail is implicitly included, and the goal is not achievable without a sub-50-door motion. The VP mandate effectively includes building one.
- If 25% is "of our addressable market (100+ doors)" — the goal is doable through the current motion. The long tail is a separate strategic question.
- If no one has defined the denominator clearly — that is the first thing to clarify, directly with Ben.

**Resolve this in week one.** The answer changes the shape of the job.

## The self-serve opportunity

UP does not have a working acquisition motion for sub-50-door PMs today. The long tail represents the majority of PMs by count. A self-serve motion could in principle unlock this segment.

Whether building such a motion is a real opportunity or a distraction from higher-priority items is an open question.

A self-serve flow has been built as a moonshot. Worth pulling that work forward as the starting point if this gets taken on.

## Marketing's role in product strategy

Moonshot work — the AI vacancy caller, the move-out tracker, the utility lookup tool — is functionally product work performed informally by marketing ICs. The implicit org questions:

- Where does Darius's product authority end?
- Where does marketing's product input begin?
- If a moonshot graduates to a real product feature, who owns it?
- Is there a formal mechanism for marketing-driven product ideas, or is it ad hoc?

Not formally resolved.

## What's testable

Several of the above disagreements are empirically testable. With proper measurement infrastructure, they can be resolved in ~60 days:

- **Are returns on existing channels actually diminishing?** Cohort and channel ROI analysis.
- **Does excellent SFR/GS marketing execution drive QoQ acceleration?** Controlled tests.
- **Is the 7.8% → 25% target achievable through current motion?** Funnel modeling against the defined TAM.
- **Does the long tail (sub-50 doors) have meaningful unit economics if a self-serve motion exists?** A scoped pilot.

The disagreements do not need to stay disagreements. Whichever view is right, the measurement infrastructure to settle them is the precondition. See [Current Metrics Snapshot](/mary-playbook/operating-system/current-metrics/) for what's measured today and what isn't.
