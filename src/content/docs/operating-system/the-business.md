What partners go through from booking a demo all the way through becoming a paying, retained customer. This is the spine the rest of the playbook hangs off of.

## Entry: Website

A prospect lands on the site and indicates how many homes they manage.

- **Under 100 doors:** self-sign up flow. They get a link, complete signup, never interact with sales or account management.
- **Over 100 doors:** triggers a round robin to the next salesperson on the team.

## Demo booking (over 100 doors)

1. Zapier fires on the booking
2. Posts to the sales Slack channel with time, customer details, and company attributes
3. Fires a Segment event identifying them as a demo-booker
4. The team manually updates the customer in HubSpot and creates a Deal

A separate Segment event fires from the database when someone has actually gone through onboarding or has an account.

## Pre-demo prep

Before the demo, the sales team:

- Creates a partner portal for the prospect
- Maps a couple of listings so the demo has real data

## The sales demo

Goals of the call:

1. Make sure they understand the product
2. Overcome objections
3. Get an onboarding call scheduled on Dario's calendar

Sales logs intel from the call as a HubSpot note.

**Historical demo close rate: 60–70%. Currently closer to 55%.** The softening tracks PM software platforms (AppFolio etc.) starting to build their own utility features. 55% is still extremely high and comes from being the best in the world at this one narrow job.

## Post-demo

Manual follow-up. Next steps. Optional sales materials sent to the property management team.

## Onboarding call

- Scheduled event, 20–30 minutes
- Guides the partner through self-onboarding questions
- Technically the partner could do this on their own — it's just complicated enough that they want the help
- Also the last step of product education and Q&A before going live

**Most important step: syncing the property management software.** That sync gives us a recurring report so our system knows what upcoming or on-market properties are. Mapping these correctly is what makes the renter experience work.

## Activation: the moment they're a customer

A partner becomes a customer when one of their renters makes the first internet purchase.

**Activation rate: ~25% of homes generate an internet purchase.** A PM with 4 upcoming move-ins will see roughly 1 internet purchase we can monetize.

## Retention signals

- **Utility completion rates** (not just internet — water, electric, gas)
- **Internet purchases over time** (watching for falloff)
- **% of moves monetized**

## Two types of churn

- **Implicit:** they stop using it without telling us
- **Explicit:** they tell us they're leaving

## Where there's more room

- **Vacancy / move-out workflow.** Today we solve move-in. When tenants move out, utilities are supposed to transition back to the PM or owner, and they often don't. PMs ask about this constantly. Hard at the data and provider layer, but real product opportunity.
- **Tighter inbound attribution.** Organic produces a meaningful share of deals but has the messiest source data. UTM hygiene and a cleaner default for organic sign-ups would help.
