What's actually broken, in rough priority order. Surfacing these on purpose so they don't have to be re-discovered through trial and error.

## 1. Understanding the customer and the opportunities

Diagnostic before tactical. Open questions:

- Who the SFR and garden-style customer actually is at this stage of the market
- What the funnel looks like, where leads come from, where they fall out
- Whether product-market and channel-market fit are sufficient to support 25% market share
- Whether the long-tail (sub-50-door PMs) is a real opportunity

This is the foundation for everything else. Skipping it leads to shipping things that don't move the needle.

## 2. Tracking infrastructure

Measurement is the precondition for almost every strategic decision the team needs to make. Specific gaps:

- Channel performance tracked month-over-month
- Campaign-level ROI
- Diminishing returns measurement (does the qualitative read hold up empirically?)
- Experimentation infrastructure to test claims at scale

Both Ben and Matt expect this to be built. They will be supportive. See [Current Metrics Snapshot](/mary-playbook/operating-system/current-metrics/) for the inventory of what exists today.

## 3. Marketing-sales integration

Currently the marketing-to-sales handoff is "lob leads over the wall" — including a Slack channel that round-robins cold-email replies to BDRs. This is a V1 that never received V2 treatment.

The opportunity is a tighter growth-function operating model:

- Marketing as air cover
- Direct-mail-to-outbound coordination
- Intent-based lead surfacing in HubSpot
- BDR follow-up on marketing-driven website visits
- Shared accountability metrics

See [Marketing-Sales Handoff](/mary-playbook/playbook/marketing-sales-handoff/) for the current state and the V2 vision in detail.

## 4. Customer marketing and testimonials

Big untapped tactic, less strategic than the items above but high-leverage. Current state:

- Gabby takes phone videos of partners at conferences
- Nothing organized downstream
- No reference customer program
- No door-count-matched testimonials for BDRs to deploy

Candidate for a quick early win. The raw material exists; nobody has built the system.

## 5. Multi-touch coordination

Partial overlap with marketing-sales integration. Includes:

- Coordinating direct mail with outbound timing
- Tracking when direct mail drives website visits
- Surfacing engaged accounts in HubSpot for BDR follow-up

## What's explicitly out of scope today (but probably shouldn't be)

Marketing currently does not own:

- **Brand**
- **PR**
- **Product marketing** (attempted with tenant liability insurance and bulk internet launches; no muscle developed)
- **Customer marketing** (Cassie on AM handles retention, but it does not operate as a growth function)
- **Community**

All of the above could reasonably fall under marketing — or, more accurately, under growth.

## What not to pursue in the first 90 days

- **LinkedIn ads.** "Fool's gold" given UP's audience and economics. Don't burn early credibility on this.
- **Major list expansion.** Significant work has already gone into scraping every meaningful PM list. Diminishing returns from here. Better to extract more value from the lists UP already has.

## What you can freely change

Anything. The team norm is to push back constructively, not defer. Expect engagement, not flattery.

The autonomy the current marketing team has is not protected. Disciplining reporting and focus is welcomed.
