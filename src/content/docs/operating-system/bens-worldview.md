How Ben thinks shapes how marketing actually gets to operate at UP. Internalize this before pitching him anything.

## The two-year destination

Ben thinks two years out. The destination is:

- **Home buyers** as the primary customer base (highest LTV, especially via supplier conversion)
- **Multifamily** as a meaningful segment (requires a different sales motion than the current SFR BDR work)
- **Becoming the electric and gas supplier** in deregulated states as the long-term monetization

Fundraising is the immediate enabler. A successful raise → more capacity → MF sales team → multifamily acquisition.

**SFR is the cash engine that funds the journey, not the destination itself.** Ben sees SFR as "more or less solved" — the BDR org will scale and capture more of the market without requiring transformational marketing investment.

## Day to day

Ben does not spend significant mental energy on marketing. He's focused on bigger strategic plays. Specifically:

- He likes initiative on moonshots, but wishes more time went to core work
- He believes excellent SFR/GS marketing execution can drive quarter-over-quarter acceleration of growth
- He thinks D2C is a 50x larger opportunity than SFR/GS and should be a major focus
- He treats vacancy-style product features as side projects, not core roadmap, given product capacity constraints
- He prioritizes D2C over SFR/GS deep penetration

## The structural insight

**There is no executive peer who represents marketing today.**

The senior marketing ICs operate on a different plane than Ben. They have candid conversations with him, but the framing gap is real:

> "We're working on our corner of the house, and he's looking at other houses to buy. So when we come to him and say, 'Look at what we can do in our little corner,' he's 'Thanks. That's just not relevant to me.' But if somebody's on his plane of house hunting, they can frame our corner work in his terms."

The VP's meta-job is translation. The corner work is the same work; the bridge to executive priorities is what having a VP at the table actually unlocks. **Every marketing initiative needs a CEO-level framing to survive contact with Ben.**

## Differing views on the slope of returns

Ben and the marketing team agree that SFR/GS marketing matters. The working views on the **slope of returns** from marketing investment differ.

- **Ben's read:** better marketing execution → quarter-over-quarter acceleration.
- **Marketing's read:** returns on existing channels are flattening; product differentiation (specifically a vacancy / move-out feature set) is required for the next leg of growth.

Neither view is currently grounded in measurement. Channel returns appear to be diminishing qualitatively, but it's not measured. **This is testable.** See [Open Questions & Big Decisions](/mary-playbook/operating-system/strategic-disagreements/).

## The discipline gap

Both Ben and Matt (head of finance) want measurement discipline from the VP. They want:

- Channel performance tracking, month-over-month
- Campaign-level ROI
- The ability to settle empirical questions with data rather than opinion

The discipline gap is acknowledged internally. Mary's measurement infrastructure work will be supported by Ben and Matt from day one.

## How to operate with Ben

- **Frame marketing work in his terms:** 2-year horizon, home buyers, fundraising, MF unlock, supplier conversion.
- **Don't bring tactical wins for their own sake.** Tie them to the broader vision.
- **Bring measurement, not impressions.**
- **Respect his autonomy preference.** He doesn't want to be in the weeds.
- **Bring hard news before it becomes urgent.**
- **Expect anything not framed in his terms to not get the attention it deserves.**
