The five formulas and rubrics you'll use over and over. Each is short on purpose. Bookmark this page.

## 1. Partner sizing (back-of-napkin)

Estimating PM size without asking:

```
Total doors ≈ Active listings × 14
```

Where listings come from their public site, Zillow, or whatever they syndicate to. Not perfect (absorption rates vary by market) but directionally right.

**Use it for:** sizing a prospect before a demo, prioritizing outbound lists, modeling pipeline value.

## 2. Revenue per partner

```
Monthly revenue = (doors / 30) × $200 × 0.25
```

Where:
- `30` = average lease length in months
- `$200` = revenue per internet purchase
- `0.25` = purchase rate (% of moves we monetize)

**Rule of thumb (Zach's):** at current conversion and turnover rates, we earn ~$1 in recurring monthly revenue per door activated.

**Use it for:** forecasting partner value, channel ROI math, owner-facing pitch math.

## 3. CAC and the allowable spend

```
CAC = (marketing spend + sales spend + onboarding spend) / activated doors
```

Track per door, not per partner. A 100-door partner and a 1,000-door partner aren't worth the same.

**Allowable CAC at 3:1 LTV ratio:** ~$2,000 all-in.
**Current actual CAC:** ~$261 blended (Zach Jan 2025; pull current).
**Headroom:** roughly 7x.

**Use it for:** channel investment decisions, justifying new bets, defending a budget ask to Ben.

## 4. LTV math

```
LTV = (monthly revenue) × (retention months)
```

Assumptions:
- **Retention:** 18 months baseline. Conservative — bigger partners do better.
- **Monthly revenue:** uses the formula above.
- **Levers that move LTV:** higher margin (operational efficiency), higher revenue per partner (new monetization, better attach), longer retention.

**Current average PM LTV:** just under $6,000.

## 5. MQL → deal math

```
Expected deals = MQLs × MQL-to-demo % × demo-to-close %
```

Conversion baselines (Q1):
- **MQL → Demo set:** ~28% SFR/MF, ~25% GS
- **Demo → Closed Won:** historically ~70%, currently ~55%

Quick mental model:
- 100 SFR MQLs → ~28 demos → ~15 closes
- 100 GS MQLs → ~25 demos → ~14 closes

**Use it for:** setting MQL targets that ladder up to deal targets, forecasting quarter pacing, sizing a campaign before launching.

## 6. Seasonality

```
Adjusted revenue = baseline revenue × seasonal multiplier
```

The curve:
- **Jan–May:** < 100% (drag)
- **May–Sep:** > 100% (peak)
- **Fall:** ≈ 100%
- **December:** lowest point

**Use it for:** forecasting honestly, not confusing seasonal lift with partner growth, picking the right month to launch a partner activation push (target March–April to capture full peak season).

## 7. Channel grading rubric

When evaluating a channel — existing or new:

1. **Activated doors per dollar.** Not MQLs, not demos. Activated doors are the only thing that pays the bills.
2. **Time-to-evidence.** How long until you can tell if it's working? Email = days. Mail = weeks. SEO = months.
3. **Floor on quality.** Can the channel produce junk (Meta with Audience Network)? If yes, what's the policing cost?
4. **Compounds or one-shot?** SEO, content, brand compound. Cold email is one-shot per send.
5. **Does Mary own it or does it own Mary?** Channels that require constant intervention to keep running drain leadership attention. Channels that self-sustain free it.

A channel doesn't have to win on all five. It does have to be honest about which ones it loses on.

## 8. Reply pattern shorthand (email outbound)

Used in the MQL engine classifier and worth carrying in your head when reviewing reply data:

| Reply pattern | What it usually means |
|---|---|
| Interested + question about cost | Hook landed. Send the example. |
| Interested + asks how revenue works | "How do you make money" — 22% of all questions. Preempt this in copy. |
| Not Interested + "we have a vendor" | Worth a competitive call-out follow-up (see [Objections](/mary-playbook/playbook/objections-and-competition/)). |
| Wrong person | Treat the response as a referral request — ask who handles utilities. |
| OOO | Re-send when they're back. Don't drop. |
| Pure unsubscribe | Honor and remove. Never re-send. |
