## Pipeline share, year over year

Where pipeline actually comes from has shifted materially over the last three years. The team grew, the BDR org scaled, and marketing's share has compressed even as marketing volume has grown.

| Year | BDR / Sales | Marketing (all channels combined) | Conferences | Other |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2024 | Minimal | Vast majority | Dominant inside marketing | — |
| 2025 | Ramping (2 → 40 BDRs) | Still meaningful share | Blended with mailers, email, influencer | — |
| **2026 (directional)** | **~70%** | **~15%** | **~15%** | — |

These are directional, not audited. The BDR org started 2025 with 2 reps, finished 2025 with 40, and is mid-2026 at 50–60. That growth is what changed the shape of pipeline.

## What this means

- **BDR/Sales is the dominant pipeline source by a wide margin.** It's also, from what we know, the highest CAC per door and the longest payback of any channel. Volume and efficiency at the team scale have surpassed anything marketing has produced on a per-channel basis.
- **Conferences punches above its weight.** ~15% of pipeline from a relatively small set of events.
- **Marketing channels individually are 1–8% each.** None dominate. The marketing mix is genuinely a mix.
- **Organic is 5–7% and ~half of it is null-attribution.** Tighter inbound attribution would shift this number up materially.

## Channels we don't run yet

- **SEO / content.** Organic produces deals but attribution is messy. No content engine. ~5–7% of pipeline already attributed to organic — a real content motion likely lifts this materially.
- **Influencer marketing (systematic).** Peter Lohmann is the existing relationship and drives a meaningful share of the partner/influencer 4–6%. No systematic program beyond him.
- **PR.** Zero owned. The TenantCloud $1M+ ARR win is an obvious story we haven't told publicly.
- **Webinars / virtual events.** Untested.
- **Reseller / agency channel.** A few PM consultants exist who could resell. Untested.

## Channel grading principle

From the operating system: **goal is most doors for the cheapest price.** Watch for channels that drive demos but don't convert to activation. Always look at the activated stage, not just demos.

The BDR org dominates volume but is the highest CAC per door and longest payback. Marketing channels are smaller share but often more efficient per door. **The interesting strategic question is not "marketing vs. BDR"** — it's "which mix produces the best blended payback at the scale we want."

Today we track cost per MQL well and cost per activated door inconsistently. That's a measurement gap, not a strategy gap. See [How We Measure](/mary-playbook/playbook/how-we-measure/) and [Current Metrics Snapshot](/mary-playbook/operating-system/current-metrics/).

## Where there's more room

- **Tighter inbound attribution.** Organic is 5–7% of pipeline with messy source data. UTM hygiene and a cleaner default for organic sign-ups would shift this number up and inform investment.
- **Air-cover coordination.** Marketing channels feed the BDR org indirectly — warmed accounts close better. Today that handoff is uncoordinated. See [Marketing-Sales Handoff](/mary-playbook/playbook/marketing-sales-handoff/).
- **A content motion.** No SEO / content engine exists. Cheapest-per-door channel in the long run, slowest to build.
- **Reframe the BDR-vs-marketing tradeoff as blended payback.** Today the two are reported and managed separately. They serve the same funnel.
