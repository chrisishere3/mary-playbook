How leads currently flow between marketing and sales. The BDR-to-AE handoff is clean. The marketing-to-BDR handoff is the V1 that never got V2.

## The sales org

- **John Raley** — VP of Sales. Owns the BDR org through Steve.
- **Steve** — BDR leader, in person in the Guadalajara office. Manages team leads directly.
- **Team leads** — Each manages a team of 4–7 reps. Responsible for training, onboarding, and day-to-day performance.
- **BDRs** — Outbound sales reps. Run the cold call and email motion against lists provided by marketing.
- **AEs** — Account Executives. Take handoffs from BDRs once a demo is booked, run the demo and close.

It covers Demo Script, Discovery, Arming Champions, Competitors, End of Day Prep, Address Upload Process, Video Library, Software Sync, Current ISPs, Weekly Demo Review, and AE Reviews. **Ask John for access on day one.**

## How leads currently flow

### BDR-sourced (the dominant motion)

1. Marketing provides target account lists, segmented by door count and other criteria
2. BDRs do outbound — cold email and cold call — through Smartlead and the GDL call team
3. When a prospect responds or books, the BDR qualifies and books a demo
4. The AE runs the demo, follows up, and closes

This produces the significant majority of demos and revenue.

### Marketing-sourced (the gap area)

Replies and engagements driven by marketing campaigns (cold email, direct mail, content) are currently distributed via a **Slack channel that round-robins to BDRs.** V1 that never received V2 treatment.

What this looks like in practice:

- Marketing campaign generates a website visit, form fill, or email reply
- The lead lands in HubSpot
- A Slack notification fires in a public channel
- The next BDR in rotation claims it and follows up

Limitations:

- No prioritization by lead quality or intent
- No coordination with concurrent outbound on the same account
- No standing visibility for BDRs into which accounts marketing is touching
- No closed-loop reporting on which marketing-sourced leads convert
- "V1 that never got a V2 treatment"

### Title and D2C motions

Sit outside the BDR motion. Title is operated through direct relationships and onboarding work (Sarah). D2C runs through self-serve from Google Ads to the UtilityWallet front-end.

## Where it works

- The BDR-to-AE handoff itself is clean. Demos book, AEs run them, conversions happen.
- The AE playbook in Base44 is a strong operational asset.
- Outbound script and offer are well-developed for SFR PMs on the major PMS platforms.
- John, Steve, and the team leads have a working operating cadence.

## Where it doesn't

- The marketing-to-BDR handoff is informal and lossy.
- BDRs do not have intent signals from marketing — no notification when a target account hits the website, downloads content, or replies to an email after their initial outbound.
- Direct mail is not coordinated with outbound timing.
- The same account can be touched by multiple channels (email, direct mail, conference) with no orchestration.
- Marketing-sourced leads are not tagged or tracked in a way that lets either team measure marketing's pipeline contribution.

## What a V2 looks like

The opportunity is a tighter growth-function operating model:

- **Marketing as air cover.** Direct mail, content, and digital touches create awareness and engagement; BDRs follow up on warmed accounts rather than cold ones.
- **Account-level coordination.** Marketing and sales target the same accounts in coordinated waves rather than parallel uncoordinated outreach.
- **Intent surfacing in HubSpot.** Lead scoring updates when marketing-driven engagement happens, so BDRs prioritize warm accounts.
- **Closed-loop attribution.** Every marketing-sourced lead is tagged through to closed-won so contribution can be measured.
- **Shared accountability metrics.** Marketing and sales both have visibility into shared goals (MQLs, opps, won deals) rather than separate dashboards.
