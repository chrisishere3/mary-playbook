The sales team runs the full pitch on a 30-minute demo. The verbatim demo script lives in the AE Playbook. For marketing, the relevant view is how the pitch is structured — because every channel, every email, every ad is a piece of the same arc.

## The arc

1. **Hook.** Either the pain ("do you confirm utilities?") or the surprise ("you can earn revenue on this?").
2. **Discovery.** Sales-side. Maps how their current process works, who owns it, where it breaks.
3. **Demo.** Shows the tenant experience and the PM admin dashboard. Ties everything back to what discovery revealed.
4. **Close.** Revenue framing + onboarding call booked on the spot.

Marketing's job is to deliver the prospect to the demo already half-sold. The pitch isn't only what sales says — it's everything they read, watched, or heard before the call.

## Discovery (the sales-side frame)

Discovery is the foundation of every demo. From the AE Playbook:

> Discovery is the foundation of your entire demo. The questions you ask at the beginning determine how relevant, personalized, and effective your presentation will be.
>
> Your goal is simple: understand how their operation actually runs today, specifically around utilities, and use that insight to position the platform in a way that feels tailored, not generic.

Areas every AE walks away understanding:

**Tenant utility process**
- Do they hold keys until utilities are confirmed?
- Do tenants need to send proof of setup?
- How is confirmation received (email, phone, screenshots)?

**Utility setup & communication**
- Do they provide a list of utility providers?
- Is it standardized or property-specific?
- Who determines which utilities service each address?

## The demo (structure)

The demo is the core sales tool. Structure:

1. **Tenant flow.** Walk through the branded tenant experience exactly as a renter would see it.
2. **Pause for sentiment.** "Do you have any quick questions or what are your overall thoughts on the tenant utility flow?"
3. **Admin dashboard.** Show the confirmation view from the PM side.
4. **Sync explanation.** How their PM software connects (or doesn't), and what that looks like operationally.
5. **Revenue and close.** "There is no cost — it's essentially like we are paying you to help streamline your services."

Sales follows the verbatim script in the AE Playbook for each section. Marketing's job is to mirror this structure in every asset: lead with the tenant flow story, surface the admin dashboard moment, end with revenue.

## Social proof we use

- **TenantCloud added $1M+ in annual revenue.** The flagship API partner case study. Mark DeHaan's quote is on file in `~/Downloads/` as PDF.
- **100K doors activated** as a team milestone (referenced in AE reviews as company-level proof).
- **60–70% historical demo close rate, ~55% current.** Used as proof of fit when prospects are skeptical.
- **NARPM and IMN trade association membership** as a credibility signal in cold outreach to those audiences.

## Where social proof is thin

- **Named PM logos.** We have hundreds of partners. We rarely show them in marketing assets.
- **Tenant testimonials.** The renter experience is a value prop but we don't have renter quotes anywhere public.
- **Concrete revenue numbers from PMs.** "$X added to our P&L last year" from a real partner would be more powerful than the TenantCloud number for the SFR audience.
- **Demo recordings (clipped).** The AE Playbook has a "video library" but the public/marketing-facing version doesn't exist yet.

## Where there's more room

- **A public case study library.** One per audience (SFR / GS / API partner). Real PM, real number, named.
- **Reusable demo clips.** Sales already clips demo recordings for follow-up. Same clips can become assets for paid social, landing pages, and email.
- **Owner-side proof.** Show the math on a real partner's portfolio: doors × move-ins × $30–50 = revenue per year. Specific, named, not modeled.
