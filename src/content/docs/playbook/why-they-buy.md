PMs buy for one of two reasons. Most close on a combination of both. Everything in the playbook ladders back to these.

## The pain: confirmation chaos

When a tenant moves in, utilities need to be in their name. The PM is on the hook to confirm this happened. In practice that means chasing tenants by phone, email, and screenshot, often for days, sometimes holding keys until proof shows up.

It's a tax on the ops team. Repetitive, doesn't scale. PMs hate it but most have accepted it as "just the job."

**The hook we lead with:**

> Do you have to confirm if tenants setup utilities?

When the answer is yes, the conversation usually goes somewhere.

## The revenue angle (not a pain — a hook)

We pay PMs $30–50 per move-in when tenants set up utilities through us. A PM operating without us isn't bleeding money in any way they feel, so this isn't a pain we relieve. But the offer is unexpected enough — "you'll *pay* me?" — that it works as a strong top-of-funnel hook.

**The hook:**

> Do you earn anything when tenants sign up for internet?

Almost always no, and the surprise opens the door. Once engaged, the conversation has to shift to the actual pain — revenue alone doesn't close.

## How it was solved before us

Call centers. Utility concierges — companies with human agents who walk tenants through setup over the phone. The model worked but it's expensive, slow, and the PM still has to manage a vendor relationship.

We built the tech-first version of the same job. Self-serve, white-labeled, automated. No call center on either end.

## Jobs to be done

The PM is hiring us to:

1. **Confirm utilities are set up** without their team chasing tenants
2. **Hand off the renter to a digital experience** that matches the brand they've built
3. **Earn a small recurring revenue stream** that they didn't have before
4. **Reduce ops headcount or repurpose it** to revenue-generating work

The tenant is hiring the platform to:

1. **Find out what utilities they're responsible for** without making phone calls
2. **Set up everything in one place** without leaving the experience
3. **Not get upsold** TV, security, moving services, satellite

## Value props for PMs (the menu)

- Zero cost (free)
- No chasing tenants
- Real-time confirmation status
- Passive revenue ($30–50 per move-in)
- 15-minute setup
- No contracts

## Value props for tenants (the menu)

- No pressure sales calls
- Self-serve portal
- See all utility options
- One link for everything
- No markups or fees
- Live support available

## Differentiators (always worth restating)

- **Self-serve.** Not concierge. No call center on either end.
- **White-labeled.** Tenant sees the PM's brand.
- **Utilities only.** No insurance, TV, moving services bundled in.
- **Contract-free.** Easy out if it doesn't work.

## Partner pitch (different audience, different framing)

For API integration partners (DoorLoop, Rentec Direct, Elise AI), the framing isn't pain — it's ARPU expansion:

> Utility Profit is the only fully digital utility setup tool for rentals. We embed into rental software so renters set up utilities and internet without leaving your app. You get rev share on every internet activation. TenantCloud added $1M+ in annual revenue. Implementation is 3 days via CSV or 4 API calls embedded.

## A narrow pain, solved well

The product solves one tight thing. That's a feature, not a limitation. Being the best in the world at this one narrow job is what kept demo close rates at 60–70% for years and at ~55% today even with PM software platforms building competing features.

## Where there's more room

- **Tenant experience as the lead.** PMs care about retention and renewals. The branded, smooth tenant flow is a real story. We don't lead with it.
- **Owner-facing math.** "$30–50 per move-in × N doors × Y move-ins per year" is the angle owners care about. Marketing copy targets ops, not owners.
- **Reply-mined messaging.** Hundreds of classified replies sit in the MQL engine. "How do you make money" is roughly 22% of all questions asked in replies, but our copy still doesn't preempt it in the body.
- **The vacancy/move-out story.** Today we solve move-in. PMs ask about move-out constantly. Real product opportunity, real marketing opportunity if we ever ship it.
