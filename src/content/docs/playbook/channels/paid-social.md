---
title: Paid Social (Meta) — Canceled
description: Two campaigns in 2026, both paused. Why the channel is off, and what would have to change for us to try again.
---

We ran Meta ads twice in 2026. Both were paused. The channel is officially canceled. Worth understanding why — the lessons apply to anything paid we try next.

## V1 (March 2 to March 25, 2026) — Pixel poisoned

- SFR-only, drove traffic to `/sfr` landing page
- Seed: 4,343 contacts (HubSpot + product users), 1% lookalike
- Budget: $100/day, scaled to $115
- 4 creatives: Revenue, Pain, Social Proof, Zero Risk
- Placements: ALL (including Audience Network)

**Result: 38% spam rate.** 10 of 26 leads were junk. Generic submitter names (Info, Hello, Support), zero page views, parked GoDaddy sites as referrers. The spam came from Audience Network placements — Meta's algo learned to optimize toward spam profiles. Pixel events can't be deleted, so the campaign was unsalvageable.

## V2 (March 25 to April 6, 2026) — Killed for opportunity cost

- Same SFR focus
- Seed expanded to 74,700 unique emails
- Budget: $60/day
- All 4 creatives were Zero Risk variations (the V1 winner)
- Placements: Facebook Feed + Instagram Feed + Stories only. No Audience Network.

Small signs of working. Not killing it. Chris paused it on April 6 — not a failed experiment exactly, just not the best use of budget vs email, mailers, and Google ads.

## What we learned

- **Audience Network is a spam farm.** Never enable it. Every spam lead in V1 came from there.
- **Landing page message match matters a lot.** When we changed the LP hero from a Revenue angle to a Pain/Zero Risk angle to match the winning ad, conversion went from 3.8% to 11.3%. Same traffic, nearly triple the conversion.
- **Meta's algorithm picks a winner fast.** Within a week, the algo gave 69% of spend to one creative ("Zero Risk"). Going in with multiple hooks and a single LP creates a mismatch unless you swap the LP to match.
- **Pixel fires ≠ real leads.** V1 pixel showed 7 leads but only 3 had matching PLG submissions. Pixel fires on form submit before the redirect lands. Slow redirect or bounced user = phantom lead.
- **Small seed list = unstable lookalike.** 4,343 contacts was too thin for Meta to build a useful lookalike. V2 at 74,700 was meaningfully better but still didn't justify the spend vs other channels.

## Cost per activated door (Q1)

| Channel | $/activated door |
|---|---|
| BDR | ~$8 |
| Mailers | ~$2 |
| Email | likely <$2 |
| Meta V1 | $1.66 (but spam-inflated) |

The math looked competitive on paper. The reality, after stripping spam, was paid social didn't outperform email and mail.

## Q1 attribution

- 2 deals from Meta (1.4% of all marketing deals)
- 20 MQLs from Meta and PLG combined
- Q2 original target was 60–75 MQLs from Meta + PLG. Now paused.

## If we ever restart

Things we'd do differently:

1. **Never Audience Network.**
2. **One hook per LP**, swap LP to match the winning creative.
3. **Verify pixel leads against backend submissions.** Don't trust the pixel.
4. **Start with a large seed list** (50K+) for a stable lookalike.
5. **Anchor on cost-per-activated-door**, not cost-per-lead.

## Where there's more room

- **Other paid channels that aren't Meta.** Google ads is live as part of D2C and worth its own audit before any new paid investment.
- **Retargeting only, not prospecting.** If we ever come back to Meta, a tightly-scoped retargeting-only campaign (warm audiences from email opens, site visitors, prior demo no-shows) avoids the lookalike-quality and spam problems entirely.
