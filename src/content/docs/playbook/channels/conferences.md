## What it is

Industry events. NARPM, IMN, IREM, NMHC, NAA-affiliated events for PM. Eventually title-specific conferences for that experiment.

## Why they matter

PM is a relationship industry. Franchise networks talk, regional ops folks talk, owners talk. Conferences are where those conversations happen face-to-face. A booth chat that leads to a demo is one of the highest-converting top-of-funnel moments we have.

For the API partnership segment specifically, **conference floor demos are the most common origin story for a partnership deal.**

## How we approach them

- **Booth + product demo.** Show the actual tool, not slides. Live tenant-side preview + dashboard on a laptop.
- **Sales team in the booth.** Jonathan, Ben, and Chris work the floor depending on the event. Demos happen on the spot.
- **Pre-event outreach.** Targeted attendee lists go through Smartlead before the event with conference-specific copy. Goal: book meetings or floor demos in advance.
- **Post-event follow-up.** Booth conversation is the entry point. Ben or Chris follows up by email referencing the specific conversation.

## What's working

- **Targeted attendee enrichment.** We've used Apify and Bright Data to pull attendee lists and pre-qualify the room before showing up.
- **Franchise events.** Keyrenter, RPM, PURE — small franchise events produce real pipeline. The network effect is strong.
- **Specific verticals.** NARPM and IREM events convert. Bigger and more generic events convert less.

## What hasn't worked

- **Big generic events.** The bigger and more generic, the worse the conversion. Sticking to vertical/franchise-specific events is the right call.

## Where there's more room

- **Conference attendee enrichment going broader.** Pre-event scraping is selective today. Could be a default play for every event we attend.
- **Speaking slots.** Most attendance has been booth + floor. Sessions, panels, and workshops haven't been pursued aggressively.
- **Post-event nurture.** Booth chats produce a pile of business cards. Follow-up is manual and probably leaks leads. A structured post-event sequence (mailer + email + LinkedIn) would compound.
- **Sponsorship vs attendance.** We've defaulted to attendance + booth. Strategic sponsorship (panel host, dinner host, after-party) is untested.
