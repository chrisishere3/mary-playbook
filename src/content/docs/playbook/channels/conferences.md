## What it is

Industry events. NARPM, IMN, IREM, NMHC, NAA-affiliated events for PM. Eventually title-specific conferences for that experiment.

## Why we go

- **Relationship-building** with target PMs and partners
- **Top-of-funnel** for direct conversations that don't happen via email
- **Industry intelligence:** what competitors are doing, what new product expectations are emerging
- **Credibility:** presence at major events signals legitimacy to prospects

PM is a relationship industry. Franchise networks talk, regional ops folks talk, owners talk. A booth chat that leads to a demo is one of the highest-converting top-of-funnel moments we have.

For the API partnership segment specifically, **conference floor demos are the most common origin story for a partnership deal.**

## Segment-by-segment fit

### SFR conferences

**Strong fit.** Our integrations cover the major SFR PMS platforms (AppFolio, Buildium, Rentvine, Propertyware), so most attendees are addressable. Our pitch lands cleanly.

### Multifamily conferences

**Weaker fit.** Many attendees are on Yardi, and until we integrate Yardi we can't serve them. We've debated specific MF conferences (Fort Worth was one recent example) and pushed back on cost-effectiveness given the integration gap.

### Title and other partner-channel events

Selectively attended. Useful if we recommit to [title](/mary-playbook/playbook/title-motion/); not a focus today.

## How we approach them

- **Booth + product demo.** Show the actual tool, not slides. Live tenant-side preview + dashboard on a laptop.
- **Sales team in the booth.** Jonathan, Ben, and Chris work the floor depending on the event. Demos happen on the spot.
- **Pre-event outreach.** Targeted attendee lists go through Smartlead before the event with conference-specific copy. Goal: book meetings or floor demos in advance.
- **Post-event follow-up.** Booth conversation is the entry point. Ben or Chris follows up by email referencing the specific conversation.

## What's working

- **Targeted attendee enrichment.** Apify and Bright Data to pull attendee lists and pre-qualify the room before showing up.
- **Franchise events.** Keyrenter, RPM, PURE — small franchise events produce real pipeline. The network effect is strong.
- **Specific verticals.** NARPM and IREM events convert. Bigger and more generic events convert less.

## What hasn't worked

- **Big generic events.** The bigger and more generic, the worse the conversion. Sticking to vertical/franchise-specific events is the right call.
- **MF-heavy events** (Yardi-dominated) — gated by the integration gap.

## Material we typically need

- Branded booth/table display materials
- One-pager handouts
- Storylane demo on tablets / laptops
- Branded swag (selective)
- A presentation deck if speaking
- A current customer list / case studies sheet for credibility

## Lessons that travel

- **Equipment can fail:** budget time and money for emergency replacement (learned in New Orleans)
- **Pre-booking matters:** walk-up booth traffic is lower-quality than pre-booked meetings
- **Local market context:** regional events benefit from local-market language and case studies

## Evaluation rubric for new conferences

1. What % of attendees fit our integration list? (Yardi-heavy events: weak fit today.)
2. What's the expected meeting density? (Booth-only events: lower. Networking-heavy: higher.)
3. What's the all-in cost (booth, travel, materials, time off other work)?
4. What's a reasonable lead/activation target to justify the spend?
5. Is there a strategic reason beyond direct ROI (e.g., partner relationships, industry presence)?

## Where there's more room

- **Conference attendee enrichment going broader.** Pre-event scraping is selective today. Could be a default play for every event we attend.
- **Speaking slots.** Most attendance has been booth + floor. Sessions, panels, and workshops haven't been pursued aggressively.
- **Post-event nurture.** Booth chats produce a pile of business cards. Follow-up is manual and probably leaks leads. A structured post-event sequence (mailer + email + LinkedIn) would compound.
- **Sponsorship vs attendance.** Defaulted to attendance + booth. Strategic sponsorship (panel host, dinner host, after-party) is untested.
